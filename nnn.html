4. Why do we use two different types of indicators in the titration?

Ans– One indicator used for carbonate and the other is used for bicarbonates.

5. Why alkalinity of water cannot be due to the simultaneous presence of OH⁻, CO₃²⁻ and HCO₃⁻, give reasons.

Ans– The possibility of OH⁻ and HCO₃⁻ ions together is ruled out because they combine instantaneously to form CO₃²⁻ ions. Thus OH⁻ and HCO₃⁻ ion cannot exist together in water and hence can be concluded that all the three OH⁻, HCO₃⁻, CO₃²⁻ ions cannot exist together.
